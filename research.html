Probe Drosohila Development

The in situ hybridization (ISH) technology reveals the spatial expression of a certain target gene with designed probe sequence producing ISH images as shown in Fig. 1. Thanks to the abundant ISH images of Drosophila embryo development published by Berkeley Drosophila Genome Project (BDGP), we are able to perform computational gene regulatory network (GRN) analysis to probe Drosophila development (example GRN of eve gene can be seen in Fig. 2.).

Fig. 1. Principles of in situ hybridization and the production of ISH images.

In the program, we propose an innovative modelling of the GRN reconstruction problem and employ a deep convolutional neural network to solve it. We compare our method with the state-of-art unsupervised GRN reconstruction method, showing at least 14% improvement in both accuracy and F1-score. By the further analysis of the reconstructed network, some intriguing findings about Drosophila eye development are revealed. Our paper is currently under peer review and expected to come soon.

Fig. 2. GRN of eve gene in Drosophila which contributes to the formation of stripes in Drosophila embryo development( ref 1 Reference 1 Wilczynski, B., & Furlong, E. E. (2010). Challenges for modeling global gene regulatory networks during development: insights from Drosophila. Developmental biology, 340(2), 161-169. ).

Fight Colorectal Cancer

The colorectal cancer (CRC) is a severe disease worldwide with its mortality rising along with the delay of diagnosis. However, due to the horribly painful process of invasive colonoscopy, few patients would like to take this examination leading to low diagnosis rate in the early state. In this program, we aim to perform the early and non-invasive diagnosis of colorectal cancer in our project. We design an engineered E.coli that will bind to T-antigen, a specific antigen expressed on the surface of colorectal cancer cells and produce unique ultrasound echo signal by expressing gas vesicle proteins (see Fig. 3). Thus, we can perform non-invasive diagnosis of colorectal cancer using ultrasound in the early state. Our experiment shows a promising future of the designed device in the clinical application. In the team, I am mainly responsible for experiment design, ultrasound machine manipulation and also later image processing.

Evaluate Neural Modulation

Recently the transcranial ultrasound stimulation has attracted increasing attention due to its advantages of high spatial resolution, deep penetration and non-invasive stimulation. However, devices dedicated for evaluation of neural modulation effect are still lacking in research and clinical applications of transcranial ultrasound stimulation. By designing a dedicated circuit for myoelectric detection and amplification, utilizing a peripherals-customized MSP430 chip and also implementing a detection algorithm of motor evoked potential (MEP) on upper computer, this study builds a platform for myoelecticity detection and analysis, satisfying the need of research and clinical applications well. We made demonstrations of our system on both simulation signal and human myoelectric signal. Experimental settings and outcomes of human myoelectric signal demonstration are shown in Fig. 4. In the two-men group, I maily deal with software design, including the control of both MSP430 chip and upper computer and also the implementation of MEP detection algorithm, while the other one is responsible for hardware development.

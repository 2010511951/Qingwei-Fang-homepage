Probe Drosophila Development

Biological Image Processing Based on Deep Learning

Deciphering the underlying gene regulatory network is a central task of developmental biology. In this program, we design an innovative computational method to reverse engineer the gene regulatory network of Drosophila's eye development. The main resources the program is based upon, the in situ hybridization (ISH) images of Drosophila embryo development from Berkeley Drosophila Genome Project (BDGP), are shown in the right picture. Details about the program can be seen in My Research Page.

Fight Colorectal Cancer

ECHO: E.coli for colon health observation

According to a research carried out by American Cancer Society(ACS), the morbidity rate of colorectal cancer(CRC) in our generation is four times higher than people born in 1950. As for morbidity, there are more and more cases of younger patients suffering CRC, clearly indicating that CRC is shifting towards younger age. As a result, we should definitely raise more awareness of this kind of disease. In this program, we aim to perform the early and non-invasive diagnosis of colorectal cancer through ultrasound imaging. Details about the program can be seen in My Research Page.

Evaluate Neural Modulation

A Electromyogram System for the Detection of Neural Modulation Effect by Transcranial Ultrasound Stimulation

Recently the transcranial ultrasound stimulation(TUS) for neuromodulation has attracted increasing attention due to its advantages of high spatial resolution, deep penetration and non-invasive stimulation. However, devices dedicated for TUS are still lacking in research and clinical applications. Therefore, designing an electromyogram system as a part of TUS system is the focus of this study. Details about the program can be seen in My Research Page.
